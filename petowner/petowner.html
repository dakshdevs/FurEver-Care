    Pet Feeding Guide

    Comprehensive nutrition guidance for every life stage

    Puppies

    Puppies grow very quickly and need nutrient-dense food for bones, muscles, and brain development. Frequent small meals prevent low blood sugar and help them stay energetic.

    • 8‚Äì12 weeks: 4 meals/day, soft or soaked puppy kibble.
    • 3‚Äì6 months: 3 meals/day, introduce balanced dry + wet food.
    • 6‚Äì12 months: 2‚Äì3 meals/day, portion by breed and weight.

    Best practice: Choose food labeled as ‚Äúpuppy formula‚Äù with added DHA for brain health.

    Tip: Avoid cow‚Äôs milk ‚Äî it can upset puppy stomachs. Stick to water and vet-approved milk replacers if needed.

    Adult Dogs

    Adult dogs require a stable, portion-controlled diet to maintain muscle tone and energy. Their needs vary by breed size, metabolism, and activity levels.

    • Small breeds: 2 meals/day (1‚Äì1.5 cups total).
    • Medium breeds: 2 meals/day (2‚Äì2.5 cups total).
    • Large breeds: 2 meals/day (3‚Äì4 cups total, divided).

    Always measure food with a cup instead of guessing. Switch to a senior diet after 7 years to support joints and digestion.

    Tip: Mix dry kibble with small amounts of wet food for taste variety, but avoid table scraps ‚Äî they cause obesity.

    Cats

    Cats are obligate carnivores ‚Äî they thrive on high-protein meals and low carbs. Indoor cats especially need portion control to avoid obesity.

    • Kittens: 3‚Äì4 meals/day with kitten-specific wet food.
    • Adult cats: 2 meals/day (dry + wet mix, 1/2‚Äì3/4 cup daily).
    • Seniors: 2‚Äì3 small meals/day, with added omega-3 for joints.

    Wet food helps hydration since cats naturally drink less water. Always keep fresh water bowls available at different spots in the home.

    Tip: Avoid feeding cats dog food ‚Äî it lacks taurine, which is essential for their heart and vision.
